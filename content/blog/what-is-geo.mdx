Search is changing faster than most businesses realise. A growing share of people no longer scroll through blue links — they ask an AI assistant a question and act on whatever the AI recommends. If your website is not appearing in those recommendations, you are losing customers to competitors who are.

That is what Generative Engine Optimisation — GEO — is designed to fix.

## What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimisation is the practice of making your website more visible to AI-powered search tools such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google's AI Overviews, and Gemini. Just as traditional SEO aims to rank well in Google's blue-link results, GEO aims to be cited, recommended, or summarised by AI assistants when they answer questions in your space.

The term was formalised in a 2023 paper from Princeton University, which found that applying certain content and technical optimisations improved a website's AI visibility by up to 40% and increased the likelihood of being directly quoted by 37%. Those numbers illustrate the opportunity — and the risk for businesses who ignore it.

## Why GEO matters now

The shift from search engines to AI assistants is accelerating:

- **37% of product discovery** now starts in an AI tool, not a search engine
- **4.4x higher conversion rates** have been observed for traffic arriving from AI recommendations compared to generic search traffic
- ChatGPT alone processes more than a billion queries per day, with a significant proportion being purchase-intent and recommendation queries

The businesses that appear in AI answers have a compounding advantage: more citations lead to more brand mentions across the web, which leads to even more citations. Early movers in GEO are building a durable competitive advantage.

<Callout type="info">
GEO is not a replacement for SEO — it is an extension of it. Good SEO practices (quality content, technical hygiene, strong backlinks) still matter, but they are no longer sufficient on their own.
</Callout>

## How GEO differs from traditional SEO

Traditional SEO focuses on signals that Google's ranking algorithm uses: backlinks, keyword density, page speed, meta tags, and hundreds of other factors. The goal is to appear in a ranked list of results.

GEO operates on a different model. AI assistants do not rank pages — they synthesise information and cite sources. The question is not "which page ranks first?" but "which sources does the AI trust and reference?"

Here is how the two disciplines compare:

| Factor | Traditional SEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|
| Target system | Search engine algorithm | Large language model |
| Goal | High ranking in results | Citation in AI answers |
| Key signals | Backlinks, keywords | Brand mentions, content authority |
| Content format | Optimised for scanability | Optimised for AI comprehension |
| Technical focus | Page speed, Core Web Vitals | robots.txt, llms.txt, schema |

## The key factors in GEO

### 1. Crawler access

AI companies send their own bots to index the web. If your robots.txt file blocks them, you will not be cited — it is that simple. Research shows that websites blocking GPTBot (ChatGPT's crawler) receive 73% fewer citations from ChatGPT. Check your robots.txt and ensure you are not inadvertently blocking AI crawlers.

### 2. llms.txt

A relatively new standard, `llms.txt` is a plain-text file placed at your domain root (e.g., `yourdomain.com/llms.txt`) that provides AI systems with a structured summary of what your site is about, your key pages, and any important context. Think of it as a `robots.txt` for AI — a direct signal about your site's content and purpose.

### 3. Structured data (schema markup)

Schema markup gives AI assistants machine-readable context about your content. Websites using appropriate schema are cited by AI tools 3.2 times more often than those without it. Relevant schema types include `Organisation`, `Product`, `FAQ`, `HowTo`, `Article`, and `BreadcrumbList`.

### 4. Content structure

AI models are trained on vast amounts of text. They respond well to content that is clearly structured, factually grounded, and written in plain language. Avoid vague or overly promotional writing. Use headings, lists, and short paragraphs. Include specific facts and data points — models are more likely to cite a sentence like "conversion rates improved by 4.4x" than "conversion rates improved significantly."

### 5. Brand mentions

Brand mentions across authoritative sites correlate with AI visibility at 0.664 — significantly higher than backlinks at 0.218 according to an Ahrefs study. When other reputable sources mention your brand in context, AI assistants learn to associate your name with your category. PR, guest content, and partnerships that generate brand mentions directly improve your GEO standing.

### 6. Content freshness

AI systems appear to favour recently updated content. Research shows that 76.4% of ChatGPT's most-cited pages were updated within the previous 30 days. Keeping your key pages current — not just publishing new posts — is an active part of GEO maintenance.

## The GEO audit

Before optimising, you need to understand where you stand. A GEO audit typically covers:

- Can AI crawlers access your site?
- Do you have an llms.txt file?
- Is structured data implemented correctly?
- Does your content clearly communicate what you do, who you serve, and why you are credible?
- Are there brand mentions on external, authoritative sites?

## Getting started

GEO is not a one-time task — it is an ongoing discipline. The good news is that many of the fundamentals (clear content, good technical hygiene, brand authority) are things you should be doing anyway.

Start with the basics: check your robots.txt, add an llms.txt, implement schema, and audit your content for clarity and specificity. Then measure. Run your site through an AI visibility test to establish a baseline, and track how your score changes as you make improvements.

The businesses that invest in GEO now will be significantly better positioned as AI assistants become the default interface for product discovery — and that transition is already underway.
